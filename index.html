About Alec

I am currently pursuing a dual degree at the University of Utah: an Honors Bachelor of Science in Biological Chemistry and a Bachelor of Music in Piano, a course of study that reflects a deliberate and rigorous engagement with both the sciences and the humanities. This interdisciplinary academic foundation supports my commitment to a liberal education rooted in analytical precision, artistic discipline, and the integration of diverse modes of inquiry.

My professional and academic development has been shaped by meaningful experiences in laboratory research, postsecondary teaching, and university governance. As an undergraduate researcher in a clinically oriented antibiotic research stream, I have contributed to the isolation and characterization of novel antimicrobial compounds. Concurrently, I have served as a Senator in the Associated Students of the University of Utah, where I have worked to shape institutional policy, represent student interests, and support equitable access to academic resources. My role as a Science Ambassador for the College of Science has further allowed me to mentor first-year students, lead outreach initiatives, and promote scientific literacy in both university and community contexts.

My long-term aspiration is to enter the field of medicine, integrating compassionate care, interdisciplinary reasoning, and ethical leadership. I am committed to leveraging my dual-degree background in Biological Chemistry and Piano to approach challenges with both analytical precision and creative problem-solving. I aim to contribute to advancements in healthcare by combining my scientific expertise with a deep understanding of the human experience, fostering innovation and empathy in equal measure.

Researcher – University of Utah

January 2025 - Present

As an undergraduate researcher in the Antibiotic Discovery stream at the University of Utah, I contribute to the search for novel antimicrobial compounds by isolating and characterizing soil-derived bacteria using culture-dependent methods. This research involves selective enrichment techniques, high-throughput screening against ESKAPE pathogens, and bioassay-guided fractionation to identify active compounds. Through this experience, I've developed hands-on proficiency in microbiology and molecular biology techniques, while contributing to clinically relevant research aimed at combating antibiotic resistance.
